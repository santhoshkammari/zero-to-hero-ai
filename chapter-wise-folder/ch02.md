## Key Decisions
1. **Probability & Statistics merge**: These are deeply intertwined in ML practice. MLE, MAP, Bayesian inference, hypothesis testing, and distributions all belong together.
2. **Information Theory stays separate**: Despite being covered briefly in the old Probability section, information theory (entropy, cross-entropy, KL, mutual information, Fisher information) is important enough and cohesive enough to warrant its own section.
3. **Numerical Computing stays**: Floating-point arithmetic, numerical stability, sparse computing, and mixed precision are practical daily-use knowledge for ML engineers. Not "nice to know" — need to know.
4. **All sections get checklists**: Every section now ends with "What You Should Now Be Able To Do" (5-10 concrete, testable items).
5. **Nav labels fixed**: Old "Probability Puzzles and Brain Teasers" label corrected to "Numerical Computing". Old "Mathematical Notation and Preliminaries" shortened to "Mathematical Notation".
