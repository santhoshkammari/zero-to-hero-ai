# Chapter 10 — Sequence Models & Attention

## Status: ✅ v3 COMPLETE — Variable-depth, concept-driven, conversational tone

## Author Intent
1. **This is the most important chapter for modern ML** — reader finishes understanding how attention works mechanically, the transformer architecture deeply, BERT vs GPT trade-offs, and why this architecture dominates.
2. **Historical context is brief** — RNN/LSTM/GRU are taught for foundations, not mastery. The real depth goes to transformers.
3. **Mechanical understanding** — not just "attention pays attention to relevant parts" but the actual Q/K/V math, multi-head mechanics, positional encoding, why scaling by √d_k matters.
4. **Two paradigms clearly compared** — BERT (bidirectional, understanding) vs GPT (autoregressive, generation). When to use which.
5. **Reality-first on emerging tech** — SSMs/Mamba covered honestly: promising but transformers still dominate. Don't bet the farm.

## Key Narrative Arc
RNNs had a memory problem → LSTM added gates → GRU simplified → Seq2Seq had a bottleneck → Attention solved it → Self-attention removed the encoder-decoder requirement → Transformers parallelized everything → BERT went bidirectional → GPT went autoregressive → SSMs offer linear alternatives → Efficient architectures make it all scale.
