# Safety & Governance Research Summary

## EU AI Act
- Entered force Aug 2024, staggered compliance
- Feb 2025: banned practices enforced + AI literacy obligations
- Aug 2025: GPAI obligations start
- Aug 2026: High-risk full compliance
- Aug 2027: Legacy high-risk embedded in regulated products
- Risk tiers: Unacceptable, High, Limited, Minimal
- Penalties: up to €35M/7% for banned; €15M/3% for high-risk non-compliance
- GPAI with >10^25 FLOPs = systemic risk → extra requirements
- Brussels Effect: de facto global standard

## US AI Policy
- Biden EO 14110 (Oct 2023): comprehensive safety-focused
- Trump revoked Jan 20, 2025; signed EO 14179 Jan 23, 2025
- Shift from safety/oversight to pro-innovation/deregulation
- Dec 2025: additional EO for national framework, federal preemption of state laws
- NIST AI RMF remains influential: Govern, Map, Measure, Manage

## Global Regulatory Landscape
- China: centralized, state-driven, mandatory algorithm registration
- UK: principles-based, decentralized, sector-specific
- Canada: AIDA (risk-based, in development)
- Key difference: EU prescriptive vs UK flexible vs US deregulatory vs China state-control

## Compute Governance
- US export controls on advanced chips + AI model weights (Jan 2025)
- 10^26 operations threshold for model weight controls
- 18 "AI Authorization Countries" exempt
- Open-weight models exempted
- Geopolitical tool in US-China tech competition

## AI Incidents
- OECD AI Incidents Monitor (AIM) launched 2023
- 56.4% increase in documented incidents 2023→2024 (149→233)
- Types: hallucination 38%, bias 24%, privacy 18%, harmful content 14%
- Notable: ChatGPT legal hallucinations, deepfake celebrity ads

## Open vs Closed Model Debate
- Open: transparency, innovation, crowdsourced safety BUT misuse risk
- Closed: control, compliance, staged releases BUT concentration of power
- Trend: tiered access based on capability, not binary open/closed

## AI Ethics Boards
- Google ATEAC dissolved in one week (2019)
- Google fired Timnit Gebru, Margaret Mitchell
- Microsoft AETHER Committee criticized for lack of teeth
- Common criticism: advisory-only, no real power, ethics-washing
- Push for external independent oversight
