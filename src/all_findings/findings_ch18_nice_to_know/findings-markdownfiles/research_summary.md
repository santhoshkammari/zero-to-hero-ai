# Ch18 Nice to Know - Research Summary

## AI Alignment Problem
- Challenge of ensuring AI goals align with human values as systems become more capable
- Forward alignment (design/training) vs backward alignment (monitoring/auditing)
- Key debate: near-term harms vs speculative long-term catastrophic risks
- Defense-in-depth approaches favored but unclear if independently protective
- Not just technical—societal and governance challenge

## Existential Risk Debates
- Paperclip maximizer thought experiment (Bostrom): AI optimizing for wrong objective
- Instrumental convergence: self-preservation, resource acquisition as sub-goals
- Split between "focus on current harms" camp vs "x-risk deserves priority" camp
- RAND, Brookings, academic institutions all engaged in debate

## Weaponized AI / LAWS
- No universal definition of lethal autonomous weapons systems
- UN GA resolution Dec 2024: 166 in favor of regulation
- Real-world use in Ukraine, Gaza conflicts
- Spectrum: human-in-loop → human-on-loop → human-out-of-loop
- Debates: ban vs regulate vs existing law sufficient

## Deepfake Governance
- Detection is arms race—generation improves faster than detection
- C2PA: cryptographic provenance metadata at capture time
- EU AI Act requires transparency for AI-generated content

## Algorithmic Accountability
- EU AI Act: risk-based classification (unacceptable/high/limited/minimal)
- Fines up to €35M or 7% global turnover
- Human oversight requirements for high-risk systems
- Implementation 2024-2026

## AI Labor Displacement
- 85M jobs displaced, 97M created (2024-2030 estimates)
- Cognitive/white-collar work now affected—genuinely new
- 70% of exposed workers can adapt; ~6M clerical workers face high risk
- Historical precedent (ATMs, spreadsheets) may not apply this time

## Environmental Justice
- US data centers: 105M tons CO2, 2.18% of national emissions
- 56% of data center electricity from fossil fuels
- Water use: 731-1125M cubic meters annually for AI by 2030
- Disproportionate burden on communities near coal-heavy grids
- Companies shifting to SE Asia—less regulatory oversight

## Indigenous Data Sovereignty
- CARE Principles: Collective benefit, Authority to control, Responsibility, Ethics
- Counterbalance to FAIR principles (technical focus only)
- Data mining parallels historical colonial resource extraction
- GIDA and Indigenous Navigator championing framework globally

## AI Consciousness / Moral Status
- No empirical evidence current AI has consciousness
- Functionalist challenge: if behavior indistinguishable, precautionary consideration?
- No jurisdiction grants AI legal personhood
- Precautionary moral risk approach (Bostrom, Regan)

## AI Researcher's Responsibility
- IEEE, ACM professional codes
- Dual-use: same capability, beneficial and harmful
- Responsible disclosure, staged release
- Ethics review committees increasingly required
